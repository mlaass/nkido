# **The Hippocratic Code of Conduct**

## **I. Preamble & Philosophy**

This project is guided by a singular, ancient rule that bridges cultures and eras: **First, do no harm** (from the Western Hippocratic tradition) and **Ahimsa** (the Eastern principle of non-injury).

We are a community of builders. Our shared goal is to create, improve, and maintain this project. To do so effectively, we must collaborate with mutual respect. We do not care about your background, politics, or personal philosophy outside of this project; within this project, you are expected to practice basic human decency, self-mastery, and constructive collaboration.

In short: **Put forward your best behavior, and don't be an asshole.**

## **II. Core Principles**

**1\. Primum Non Nocere / Ahimsa (Do No Harm)**

Before you act, type, or submit, consider if your action harms the project, the code, or the community. Refrain from hostility, sabotage, or intentional disruption. If you cannot contribute positively in a given moment, choose to step back.

**2\. Right Speech**

Drawing from ancient Eastern philosophy, we practice "Right Speech." Before communicating, ask yourself:

* Is it true?
* Is it useful?
* Is it spoken with goodwill?

If a comment is merely venting frustration or attacking another person, it does not belong here. Criticize the code, not the coder.

**3\. Sympatheia & Ren (Interconnectedness and Benevolence)**

The Stoics believed in *Sympatheia* (mutual interdependence), and Confucians in *Ren* (humaneness). Recognize that behind every screen is a human being volunteering their time. Assume good faith. Give others the benefit of the doubt. If someone makes a mistake, correct them with the same patience you would want for yourself.

**4\. The Dichotomy of Control**

You cannot control how others act, but you have absolute control over your own reactions. When faced with disagreement, respond with reason (*Logos*) rather than pure emotion. Do not let another person's poor behavior drag you down to their level.

## **III. Expected Behavior**

To maintain a productive and virtuous environment, contributors are expected to:

* **Be Polite and Professional:** Use a courteous tone in pull requests, issues, and discussions.
* **Focus on the Work:** Keep discussions centered on the code, the architecture, and the project's goals.
* **Accept Constructive Criticism Gracefully:** Egos must be left at the door. We review code to improve the project, not to diminish the author.
* **Be Helpful:** If you see someone struggling, offer guidance. Ancient philosophers viewed teaching as a duty to the community.

## **IV. Unacceptable Behavior**

Behaviors that violate the "Do No Harm" principle will not be tolerated. These include, but are not limited to:

* *Ad hominem* attacks (insulting or attacking a person rather than addressing their argument/code).
* Trolling, baiting, or intentionally starting non-project-related arguments.
* Harassment of any kind, public or private.
* Spamming or intentionally degrading the quality of the repository.
* Holding grudges or bringing outside personal/political conflicts into the project space.

## **V. Resolution and Enforcement**

If someone violates this code, our goal is correction, not merely punishment.

1. **First Instance (Correction):** The contributor will be reminded of the Code of Conduct and asked to adjust their communication or behavior.
2. **Repeated/Severe Instances (Removal):** If a contributor repeatedly causes harm or refuses to act with basic decency, they will be blocked from the repository. The health of the project and the peace of the community outweigh the contributions of any single individual.

*Let your contributions be rigorous, and let your conduct be virtuous.*

**Reporting Issues:**

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please open an issue or contact the maintainers through [GitHub](https://github.com/mlaass/nkido).
